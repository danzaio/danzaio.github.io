Rift+ // Champion profiles

Play cleaner with Rift+

Rift+ is built around the decisions that happen throughout a game.

Finding better engage paths. Preserving cooldowns when they are not needed. Identifying safer opportunities. Reacting to threats before they become a problem. Adapting to changing situations, enemy positioning, health thresholds, and fight state.

Many of the systems found throughout Rift+ are designed around situations that do not fit into a simple combo sequence. Advanced target selection, champion-specific mechanics, farming logic, defensive reactions, kill confirmation, safety checks, utility features, and countless edge cases all play a role in how each implementation behaves.

The goal is simple: make champions feel complete.

If a mechanic is worth automating, optimizing, or improving, it is probably already here.

Why Rift+

Make champions feel complete

Profile settings

Combat planner

Champion-specific rules decide when to engage, when to hold cooldowns, how to route toward targets, and how to adapt to the current fight state.

Target and path logic

Profiles choose between direct casts, minion or jungle routes, bridge units, execute windows, and defensive paths instead of blindly pressing ready spells.

Safety checks

Health thresholds, nearby threats, enemy count, turret checks, revive states, and safe end-position validation keep high-commitment routes from firing blindly.

Manual control

Manual controls expose combo, harass, clear, flee, forced routes, turret exceptions, and high-commitment plays without hiding the underlying decisions.

Readable tuning

Extensive grouped settings make champion-specific mechanics, defensive reactions, kill confirmation, visuals, and diagnostics easy to tune.

Hook controlE magnetR executeKill secureAnti-gapclose

Pyke execution

Hooks, routes, executes

Pyke is built around creating the right pick before committing: charged Q timing, hook-only releases, tap range handling, crowd-control extension, and target whitelists all decide when Bone Skewer should actually leave his hand.

The profile preserves Q when Death from Below already executes, avoids revive or invulnerability states, supports manual R, and applies optional execute damage reduction so threshold calls stay conservative.

Phantom Undertow is not just a dash. It can follow Q hits, start when Q would collide, use magnet movement for stun alignment, avoid crowded end positions, respect ally presence, dodge dangerous spells, and answer enemy gapclosers.

If Pyke can create a cleaner pick, safer escape, or sharper execute window, the profile is designed to expose that decision.

Yasuo flow

Stacks, dashes, knockups

Yasuo is built around flow: Q1/Q2 pressure, Q3 tornado timing, automatic Q stacking, bridge dashes through minions and jungle monsters, safe end-position checks, and R decisions that wait for real airborne value.

The profile can last-hit with Q, stack Q only when enemies are not too close, route E through bridge units, use E as a dodge tool, and prevent dashes that end under turret or into unsafe positions.

Wind Wall reacts to observed enemy spells with health gates, optional delay, combat-only mode, and per-spell controls, while Last Breath checks airborne count, target HP, projected follow-up damage, waste prevention, and EQ Flash follow-up windows.

If Yasuo needs a better route, a cleaner tornado window, or a smarter Last Breath, the profile is built around that decision.
