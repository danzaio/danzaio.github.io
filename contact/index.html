Brief console

Scope the message by signal, not polish.

Keep the first note practical: what changes, who reviews it, what systems are involved, and what proof would make it safe to use.

input 1

Product or workflow goal

Tell me what you want to build or automate: AI product, internal copilot, research agent, browser automation, RAG system, or agent workflow system.

input 2

Users and business context

Share who will use it, what work is slow today, what systems it touches, and what a successful first version must change.

input 3

Systems, data, and tools

Name the APIs, browser surfaces, documents, databases, tools, and examples the system needs to read or operate against.

input 4

Human oversight

Say where people must approve, review, redact, or stop the workflow before customer data, money, credentials, or production systems are touched.

input 5

Trust target

Define what would make the system reliable enough to use: evaluation cases, reviewer checks, audit trails, browser QA, source grounding, or rollback rules.

input 6

Delivery shape

Tell me whether you need a prototype, production feature, automation runbook, agent plan, integration spec, or maintainable handoff for your team.

Privacy boundary

Use this as a local checklist before you write. Keep secrets, raw exports, and unnecessary sensitive data out of the first message.

guide

No secrets in email

Do not send credentials, tokens, cookies, keys, or recovery codes.

guide

Redact before sharing

Summaries and shaped samples are better than raw private exports.

guide

Write the approval rule

Say what must stop for a person before it affects users or production.

Do send

  • Public links, product notes, screenshots with sensitive values removed
  • Short summaries of private workflows instead of raw exports
  • Redacted samples that show shape, fields, and failure modes
  • Approval rules for anything that affects users, money, or production

Do not send

  • Credentials, tokens, cookies, private keys, or recovery codes
  • Raw sensitive exports that can be summarized or redacted first
  • Sensitive customer or employee data that is not needed for scoping
  • Irreversible production actions without a human approval rule
